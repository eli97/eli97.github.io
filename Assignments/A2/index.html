This manifested itself in the fear of the other, and culminated in the cold war which simultaneously marked the end of this paradigm as globalization ramped up and the world became increasingly interconnected. The result was a disappearance of the other as our country integrated and welcomed people of different races and religions. The concept of the other shifted to merely different, which in immunological terms translates to same, and is therefore not warded off. The disappearance of otherness continued, expanding from economics, to people of all walks of life, which is a great thing - except that it introduced a new problem. Such an absence of negativity yields an excess of positivity, and in combination with oversaturated information streams, has led to a kind of mass obesity from stuffing our minds with too much information and positivity. The immune system does not attack fat, but instead an obese individual experiences other emergent and chronic health conditions as a result of a large excess of fat.
